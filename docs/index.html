FAQ

Questions? Answered.

Is Kalam really free?

Yes — completely free and open-source for personal, educational, and non-profit use under the MIT license. Small teams of up to 2 people can use it commercially at no cost too. Larger organizations need a commercial license.

Does my audio leave my device?

Only if you choose cloud mode. In Offline mode, everything is processed locally on your machine using a Whisper model. Kalam itself never stores your audio or sends it to our servers — ever.

How does it compare to Wispr Flow?

Kalam is an open-source alternative with no subscription. Unlike Wispr Flow, Kalam supports Linux, lets you bring your own API keys, and offers full offline capability. See the comparison table above.

Which platforms are supported?

Windows 10+, macOS 11+, and Linux (AppImage and .deb). Kalam is desktop-first — no mobile apps at this time.

Do I need an API key?

Only for cloud transcription. Bring your own Groq or OpenAI key for blazing-fast cloud STT. Offline mode requires no key and no internet connection at all.

How do I start dictating?

Press and hold your hotkey (default: Right Ctrl), speak naturally, then release. Your words appear wherever your cursor is — any app, any text field. You can also use toggle mode for hands-free dictation.

Is my data safe?

Kalam stores dictation history and workspace data locally on your device. Audio is never written to disk. In Auto mode, Kalam detects sensitive apps and automatically switches to local processing. Read our full privacy policy.
