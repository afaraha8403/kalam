Frequently Asked Questions

Sleek, expanding accordion components for dense information.

Is Kalam really free?

Yes. Kalam is completely free and open-source for noncommercial use under the MIT license. You can use it personally, for education, or in a non-profit setting without paying anything.

Does my audio leave my device?

It depends on your settings. In Offline mode, everything is processed locally on your machine. In Cloud mode, audio is sent to the STT provider you configure (like OpenAI). Kalam itself never stores your audio or sends it to our servers.

How does it compare to Wispr Flow?

Kalam is an open-source alternative. Unlike Wispr Flow, Kalam doesn't require a subscription, supports Linux (in addition to Windows/Mac), and gives you complete control over your API keys and local processing options.
